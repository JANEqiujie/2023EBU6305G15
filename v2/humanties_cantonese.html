Cantonese

Cantonese concept:
Cantonese (Cantonese) is a Chinese dialect represented by Guangzhou dialect
It is mainly used in most of Guangdong Province, southeastern Guangxi Province, Hong Kong and Macao
And some overseas Chinese communities
In these areas, they call it "vernacular."

Cantonese

Cantonese Pinyin:
There is no unified phonetic system for Cantonese
The most commonly used is the Hong Kong Philological Society Cantonese Pinyin scheme
Abbreviation Cantonese spelling (English: Jyutping)
is a Romanized Pinyin transliteration scheme for Cantonese developed by the Philological Society of Hong Kong in 1993

Its purpose is to develop a simple, reasonable, easy to learn and easy to use Cantonese phonetic transcription scheme
to unify the confusion in the use of Cantonese Pinyin in all sectors of society (disunity, more than one version)
This pinyin scheme has been widely adopted in the mainland, Hong Kong, Macao and overseas
